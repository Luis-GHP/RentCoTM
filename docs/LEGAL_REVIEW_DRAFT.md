### Service Description

RentCo is a rental-management tool that helps users manage property, unit, lease, rent, payment, official receipt, utility bill, document, notification, and maintenance workflows. RentCo may also support optional, landlord-requested tenant identity verification through a third-party provider.

RentCo is not a bank, escrow service, payment processor, tax adviser, legal adviser, property broker, insurer, government agency, or dispute-resolution authority. Landlords and tenants remain responsible for reviewing records, complying with applicable lease terms and laws, and resolving disputes.

### Accounts and Roles

Users must provide accurate, current, and complete account information. Landlords are responsible for inviting the correct tenant to the correct unit and for maintaining accurate lease, rent, property, utility, and receipt records. Tenants are responsible for providing their own accurate registration, contact, and profile information when accepting an invite.

You are responsible for keeping your login credentials secure and for activity under your account, except where unauthorized access is caused by RentCo’s failure to use reasonable security measures.

### Uploaded Content

You may upload files such as utility bills, payment receipts, maintenance photos, documents, and other records needed for rental workflows. You must only upload content that you are authorized to share and that is lawful, accurate, non-malicious, and relevant to the rental relationship.

RentCo may reject, restrict, remove, or preserve content when necessary to operate the service, comply with law, investigate abuse, maintain security, or respond to disputes.

### Payments, Receipts, and Records

RentCo may help landlords and tenants record rent payments, payment receipts, official receipt details, utility bills, and proof-of-payment documents. Unless expressly stated otherwise in a separate written agreement, RentCo does not collect, hold, settle, transfer, or guarantee funds.

Landlords remain responsible for confirming whether a payment was actually received, issuing accurate official receipts where required, maintaining required tax or accounting records, and complying with applicable rent, lease, and consumer-protection laws.

Tenants remain responsible for paying rent and utility obligations under their lease and for submitting accurate proof where requested.

### Maintenance Requests

RentCo may let tenants submit maintenance requests and photos, and may let landlords mark issues as fixed, closed, or needing further action. RentCo does not inspect properties, guarantee repair quality, or determine legal responsibility for repairs. Landlords and tenants remain responsible for their legal and contractual maintenance obligations.

### Identity Verification

RentCo may support identity verification only when requested by a landlord for a tenant profile. The verification flow may be hosted by Didit or another third-party provider and may involve ID document capture, liveness checks, and face matching, depending on the configured workflow.

RentCo is intended to store verification status and limited metadata, not raw identity-document images from Didit. Verification results are not a guarantee of tenant suitability, financial capability, lawful occupancy, or absence of fraud.

### Acceptable Use

- Uploading false, misleading, illegal, infringing, harmful, or malicious content.

- Circumventing security, rate limits, storage controls, or identity-verification controls.

- Interfering with the service or using automated scraping without written permission.

### Availability and Changes

## Draft Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how RentCo collects, uses, shares, stores, and deletes personal information.

RentCo is provided by **[Legal Entity Name]**. For privacy questions or requests, contact **[privacy@rentco.app]** or **[Data Protection Officer / Privacy Contact]** at **[Business Address]**.

### Information We Collect

RentCo may collect:

- Account information: user ID, email address, password authentication data handled by the authentication provider, account role, account status, creation date, and login/session data.
- Profile information: landlord name, tenant name, contact email, phone number, and other profile details users choose or are required to provide.
- Property and lease information: properties, units, addresses, lease dates, rent amounts, deposits, tenant-unit links, and related rental records.
- Payment and utility information: rent records, payment status, payment dates, methods, reference numbers, official receipt details, utility bill details, billing periods, amounts, usage values, uploaded bills, and payment receipts.
- Maintenance information: request titles, descriptions, categories, priorities, status, photos, comments, resolution actions, and timestamps.
- Documents and files: uploaded leases, receipts, utility bills, maintenance images, and other files attached to app records.
- Notification information: device push tokens, notification events, read status, and notification metadata.
- Identity verification information: landlord-requested verification status, provider session metadata, document type, issuing country, limited decision metadata, and verification timestamps. RentCo is intended not to store raw ID-document photos from Didit.
- Technical information: logs, device/browser information, IP address where collected by service providers, error reports, security events, and usage data needed to operate and protect the service.

### How We Use Information

RentCo uses information to:

- Create, authenticate, and secure user accounts.
- Connect landlords, tenants, units, and leases.
- Manage rent, utility, receipt, maintenance, document, and notification workflows.
- Let landlords and tenants view records that belong to their rental relationship.
- Upload, store, retrieve, and secure documents and images.
- Generate receipts, parse utility bills, and support AI-assisted bill entry where enabled.
- Send app notifications and maintain notification history.
- Process landlord-requested tenant identity verification through a third-party provider.
- Provide support, investigate bugs, prevent abuse, and maintain security.
- Comply with legal, tax, accounting, audit, dispute, and regulatory obligations.

### Legal Bases and Consent

Depending on the jurisdiction and user role, RentCo may process data based on contract performance, legitimate interests, legal obligations, consent, or another lawful basis recognized by applicable law.

Where consent is required, users should receive clear notice before the relevant processing. Users may withdraw consent where applicable, but some features may no longer work if the data is necessary for the service.

RentCo may share information:

- Between landlords and tenants where necessary for the rental relationship and app workflow.
- With service providers that help operate RentCo, such as Supabase, Didit, Expo, Anthropic, Apple, Google, hosting providers, analytics/error-monitoring providers if added, and support tools if added.
- With legal, accounting, security, or compliance advisers where reasonably necessary.

## Draft Identity Verification Notice

Identity verification is not required for every tenant by default. A landlord may request identity verification for a tenant profile when the landlord believes it is necessary for the rental relationship.

When verification is requested, the tenant will see the request inside RentCo before starting. The verification session may be hosted by Didit and may involve document capture, liveness checks, face matching, and automated or manual review depending on the configured workflow.

RentCo is intended to store only verification result data and limited metadata, such as status, provider session reference, document type, issuing country, timestamps, and decision metadata. RentCo should not store raw identity-document images from Didit in RentCo storage.

Verification results help landlords confirm whether the tenant completed the provider’s verification process. They do not guarantee tenant suitability, payment ability, lawful occupancy, absence of fraud, or compliance with lease terms.

## App Store / Google Play Checklist

- Public privacy policy URL exists, works without login, is not a PDF, is not geofenced, and names RentCo or the legal developer entity.
- Public account deletion URL exists, works without login, names RentCo or the developer entity, and clearly explains how to request deletion.
- In-app account deletion initiation is easy to find in account settings or Legal & Support.
- App Store Connect privacy labels match the actual data collected by the app.
- Google Play Data Safety form matches the actual data collected, shared, encrypted, deletable, and optional/required by the app.
- Third-party service disclosures match actual SDKs, APIs, edge functions, analytics, AI providers, notification providers, and verification providers.
- Any sensitive data handling, including identity verification and uploaded documents, has clear notice and appropriate access controls.
